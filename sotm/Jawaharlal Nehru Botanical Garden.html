Overview

Jawaharlal Nehru Botanical Garden

Established in 1987, the Jawaharlal Nehru Botanical Garden is a lush and serene botanical sanctuary known for its diverse range of flora, especially orchids and rare plant species. It is situated near the famous Rumtek Monastery, around 24 kilometers from Gangtok, and offers visitors an opportunity to experience Sikkim's rich biodiversity and scenic beauty.

Key Attractions

Floral Diversity

Apart from orchids, the garden boasts an extensive variety of trees, shrubs, and flowers, including rhododendrons, magnolias, and ferns. Many of these plants are native to the Eastern Himalayas and are carefully preserved within the garden's different zones.

Flower Shows:

Twice a year, flower shows are held, which attract thousands of visitors. These exhibitions feature themed displays of flowers, exotic plants, and beautiful garden structures.

Floral Clock:

A large floral clock near the entrance, installed by HMT (Hindustan Machine Tools), adds a unique feature to the garden. The surrounding garden and statues make this a popular photo spot.

Lake and Fountains:

Lalbagh also boasts a serene lake where visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk around its perimeter. There are numerous fountains scattered across the garden, adding to its beauty.
