Overview

Government Botanical Garden

The Government Botanical Garden in Ooty is a stunning example of British colonial landscaping, spread over 55 acres. Nestled in the Nilgiri Hills at an altitude of 2,400 meters (7,874 ft), this lush garden is home to an extensive collection of indigenous and exotic plants, offering a peaceful escape for nature lovers.

Sections of the Garden

Lower Garden

Features sprawling lawns and the famous Kew Tree, brought from the Royal Botanic Gardens in Kew, London.

New Garden

"Known for its annual flower shows and vivid flower beds.

Italian Garden

Designed with Italian aesthetics, this section is notable for its colorful flowers arranged geometrically.

Conservatory

Built in 1912, houses numerous varieties of flowering plants.

Nurseries

Specializing in plant propagation and maintenance, this section includes indoor plants, ferns, and medicinal plants.

Attractions

Floral Diversity

Over 1,000 species of plants, including rare ferns, orchids, and medicinal plants.

Flower Shows

Held annually in May, this event showcases an extensive variety of vibrant flowers and attracts visitors from all over the country.

Glasshouse

A greenhouse for delicate plants, including seasonal flowers and exotic species.
