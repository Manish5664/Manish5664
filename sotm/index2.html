Themed Gardens

Rose Garden: A vibrant array of roses in various colors and fragrances. Medicinal Plants Garden: A section dedicated to plants with healing properties. Cactus House: A unique collection of cacti from around the world.

The Great Banyan Tree

One of the main attractions of the garden is the Great Banyan Tree, which is considered the world's largest tree in terms of canopy size. The tree is over 250 years old and has a canopy that covers an area of around 14,500 square meters. It looks like a forest on its own due to its extensive aerial roots.

Palm Collection

The garden has a vast collection of palm species, featuring both native and exotic varieties. The palms create an impressive display, providing an aesthetic appeal and valuable research specimens for botanists.

Lotus and Water Lily Ponds

The Central National Herbarium, housed within the garden, contains a collection of over 2.5 million dried plant specimens. It is an important resource for research and conservation efforts in India and beyond.

Rare and Endangered Plants

The botanic garden is home to a variety of rare and endangered plant species, including several indigenous to the Indian subcontinent. Efforts are made to conserve and propagate these plants as part of the garden’s conservation initiatives.

Medicinal Plants Garden

The medicinal plants section displays a wide range of plants with known medicinal properties. This collection is of educational and research value, showing the traditional and Ayurvedic uses of these plants.

Orchid House

The Orchid House has a stunning collection of native and exotic orchids. Visitors can see colorful and fragrant orchids, which bloom in different seasons, adding vibrancy to the garden.

Rock Garden

The rock garden features an array of alpine and rock-loving plants arranged artistically. This section highlights the diversity of India’s high-altitude flora.
