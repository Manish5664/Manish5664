Overview

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh Botanical Garden, covering an area of 240 acres, is a treasure trove of plants and a key landmark of Bengaluru. Initially commissioned by Hyder Ali in the 18th century and later completed by his son, Tipu Sultan, the garden is renowned for its vast collection of exotic and native plants, as well as its historical significance. It is a prime spot for morning walkers and nature enthusiasts.First planned and laid out during the dalavaiship of King Hyder Ali, the garden was later managed under numerous British Superintendents before Indian Independence. It was responsible for the introduction and propagation of numerous ornamental plants as well as those of economic value. It also served a social function as a park and recreational space, with a central glass house dating from 1890 which was used for flower shows. In modern times, it hosts two flower shows coinciding with the week of Republic Day (26 January) and Independence Day (15 August). As an urban green space along with Cubbon Park, it is also home to numerous wild species of birds and other wildlife. The garden also has a lake adjoining a large rock on which a watchtower had been constructed during the reign of Kempegowda II.

Key Attractions

Glass House:

Built in 1889, the iconic Glass House is one of Lalbagh's most famous landmarks. It hosts the biannual flower shows (Republic Day in January and Independence Day in August) that showcase elaborate floral arrangements and rare flowers.

Flower Shows:

Twice a year, flower shows are held, which attract thousands of visitors. These exhibitions feature themed displays of flowers, exotic plants, and beautiful garden structures.

Floral Clock:

A large floral clock near the entrance, installed by HMT (Hindustan Machine Tools), adds a unique feature to the garden. The surrounding garden and statues make this a popular photo spot.

Lake and Fountains:

Lalbagh also boasts a serene lake where visitors can enjoy a peaceful walk around its perimeter. There are numerous fountains scattered across the garden, adding to its beauty.
